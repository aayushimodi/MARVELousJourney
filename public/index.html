What does it take to be in Marvel?
It is the dream of many to be a superhero, specifically a Marvel superhero. So what does it take to be a part of Marvel? In this web page, we will answer this question by taking a deep dive into Marvel characters and specifically analyze the Avengers, the stars of Marvel, by investigating the various attributes, features, and strengths that make up a Marvel superhero. We hope to provide a thorough level of insight and inspiration for superfans that have dreamed of becoming their favorite Avenger and/or those who want to understand the behind-the-scenes analytics. Using data from numerous marvel characters, we explore and connect different characters' relationships, patterns, and similarities in four specific categories:
  • Superhero Attributes
  • Physical Features
  • Death Statistics
  • Good vs. Evil
Superhero Attributes
The main part of being a superhero requires having the talent to hold your own against the many supervillains that plague our world. But how would you know what is powerful enough? One of the ways is to compare your scores with that of other superheroes. We can look into the Avengers by judging their superhero attributes - Intelligence, Strength, Speed, Durability, Power, and Combat. These attributes indicate to us where the strengths of each Avenger lie, and which Avengers are stronger than others overall based on these characteristics. Physical Features

To truly embody a Marvel character, fans must understand the key physical features of each character. We have analyzed a relevant set of attributes for this, including gender, eye color, breed, and hair color, weight, and height for each character. This is further broken down into subcategories for each attribute so that we can find how many characters encompass each feature as well as what portion they make up of all the marvel characters. Through this section, we hope to highlight the growing gender, breed, weight, and height diversity among Marvel characters to the Avengers.

While there is a majority male presence in gender, it is difficult to make any conclusions as the number of women heroes has been increasing over the years according to movie releases that have occurred in the last decade. However, the conclusion can be made based on the pie chart that it seems to be easier to become a Marvel hero as a male than as a female which we infer is a correlated to the physical features weight and height. Males biologically tend to have a greater weight and height than women giving them an advantage which can be seen in the weight and height sections with majority characters having a weight of 101 - 150 pounds and a height of 101 to 150 inches (8.4 feet to 12.5). Breaking down the pie chart further, it can be noted that common eye colors of a marvel character include black, brown and blue eyes. Moreover, while most marvel characters are human, there is a significant number of mutants and Gods as Marvel heroes as well. Lastly, moving on to hair color, most characters seem to have black, brown, blond, or no hair.

Good vs. Evil

The Marvel Cinematic Universe has been defined by legendary battles that feature powerful heroes and villains. At first glance, it often seems impossible that a beloved Avenger might have any weakness compared to the opponent at hand. However, an important question to ask is if good does really triumph over evil (at least on paper). If one is to truly become a successful superhero, it is important to understand how the current Avengers stack up to villains of the past and glean how they themselves might fare in future battles.
